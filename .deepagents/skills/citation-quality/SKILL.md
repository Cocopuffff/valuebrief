# Citation And Source Quality

Use citations as evidence, not decoration.

Rules:

- Attach a source URL immediately after each major factual claim.
- Prefer primary sources: SEC filings, company investor relations, official press releases, regulatory filings, and exchange data.
- Use reputable secondary sources only when primary sources are unavailable or when the claim is about external market context.
- Do not cite search result snippets as final evidence. Scrape or open the underlying page before relying on it.
- If sources conflict, state the conflict and prefer the more primary or more recent source.
- Avoid vague phrases such as "reports say" unless the report is named and cited.
